Children’s Coverage at Risk
The coming months could see major changes in children’s health coverage:
  • The Supreme Court will decide in King v. Burwell whether premium tax credits will be available in the 34 states that have not established their own health insurance marketplaces. The federal government has established marketplaces in those states.
  • Federal funding for the Children’s Health Insurance Program (CHIP) will end this October unless Congress extends it, eliminating separate CHIP programs in 36 states.
  • Currently, states must preserve eligibility standards for children until 2019. Proposed Congressional legislation would immediately let states reduce eligibility for Medicaid and CHIP to 138 percent FPL.
